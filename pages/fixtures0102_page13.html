Hard earned point

West Middlesex Sunday Football League
Premier Division

Chiswick Albion 1 Stanwell 1

                                         
                                                          Liam White (pen)    Anthony Eggington


Both sets of players put in a sound performance in this Premier Division encounter at the White Lodge.  The Albion got of to a good start and Liam White went close.  An early goal was vital for the Albion, and it duly came when Adam White turned his defender who bought him down in the box for a penalty. Up stepped big brother Liam who gave Crowder no chance from the spot.
The Albion though was unable to keep this lead and following a throw in and a cross Anthony Eggington blasted home from close in.  A great strike from Sammy Boyd was too hot to handle for Crowder but he recovered well to turn away Adam White's follow up effort. 
Peter Sousa back in the Albion goal for the first time since October did well after a shaky start as the confidence came flooding back.This he had to be as
Stanwell pushed on looking for a top four finish in the Division. 
This was also on the minds of the Albion, but Stanwell were not easy to break down and their central defender Frost was at the heart of most of this.  Anthony Clarke now playing his best football for the Albion went close, as did Rob Ursell.  From an Ursell corner Liam White's glancing header flew just over. Stanwell
themselves maybe should have scored but their forward line did not take the chances created.  The Albion almost snatched the winner in the closing minutes as Karl Velinor hit the crossbar following a corner. This was probably just as both sides deserved a point out of a hard fought contest well refereed by Andy Davies.  Albion next week has a last chance of glory in the Upper Supplementary Cup against Hampton Village. 
The winner will play the following week in the final against The Rising Sun.
