Another League win as the Albion roll on

West Middlesex Sunday Football League
Premier Division

Hampton Village 2 Chiswick Albion 7

                                                   
                                                                          Sean Kelly                    Liam White
                                                                          Paul Drakes                  Adam White (3)

                                    Alan Sime
                                          Vojin Soskic
                                          Dani Rouco


Chiswick Albion won three valuable points against a hard working Hampton Village side, and remain in the leading pack at the top end of the table. Albion started well and went close on a couple of occasions, but failed to make the breakthrough. This came when Liam White created a chance for himself and slotted into the bottom
corner. A goalkeeping crisis on the morning of the match, with both Peter Sousa and Adrian Rouco both out of action, forced manager Mark White into selecting right back Dave Gaffney between the sticks. This looked to have been a poor choice as he fumbled a corner, and then was caught off his line by Sean Kelly from 30 yards for Village's equaliser. Both Robert Dawson and Dani Rouco both were just off target, as was Alan Sime who played a neat one two with Adam White. This was soon
rectified when Liam White showed great determination and sent the ball to Alan Sime who converted this time. Adam White then picked up a ball from Dawson on his chest went around the defender and keeper for a 3-1 half time lead.
The second half was much the same, chances missed and another gaff from Gaffney who was this time stuck on his line when a defensive header back to him let in Paul Drakes who finished well. This was a wake up call, and straight away Adam White scored for a 4-2 advantage. Richard Ralph then crossed and Adam White side footed home for his second successive hat-trick. Albion was now on a roll and Vojin Soskic volleyed home a beauty with his right foot, usually only used to stand on. The goal of the game came when Dani Rouco crashed in Dawson's corner that went in like a rocket.

Manager's Man of the Match - Adam White

Match Report by Mark White
