Season by Season highlights at a glance

2021/22  Premier Division Winners.
2020/21 Runners Up Premier Division, No domestic cups due to Covid-19
2019/20  The season halted due to Covid-19 whilst in 3rd place.
2018/19  Riverside Challenge Cup Finalists (Barnes AFC 0-2) 3rd Premier Division, Semi Finalist MCFA Premier Cup, Semi-Finalists Premier Cup.
2017/18  Premier Division Champions, MCFA Premier Cup Runners Up (NLO 3-3 pens)
Surrey Premier Cup Runners Up (Lambeth All Stars 0-2) League Cup Finalist (not played v AFC Barnes)
2016/17  Winners Riverside Challenge Trophy (Barnes Eagles Seniors 5-1) League Cup Runners Up
Semi Finalists MCFA Premier Cup, Runners Up Premier Division.
2015/16  Winners of the MCFA Premier Cup on penalties (Enfield Rangers Old Boys) and
League Cup 3-2 (Barnes Albion)
2014/15  Amalgamated with Chiswick Griffin, Semi Finalist MCFA Premier Cup. Third place in C&DSFL
Premier Division.
2013/14  Seventh placed in C&DSFL.
2012/13  Re-joined Chiswick and District League.
2011/12  Runners Up (FC Yiewsley) in the Premier Division Cup Final AET.
2010/11 Joined the Hayes Middlesex Sunday Football League, Premier Division Cup semi-finalists
2009/10  WMSFL Premier Cup Semi-Finalists.
2008/09  Re-Joined West Middlesex Sunday Football League, Premier Cup Semi-Finalists.
2007/08  Winner Chiswick & District Premier Division and League Cup (Acton & Ealing Whistlers 4-2)
Runners Up Riverside Challenge Trophy (Chiswick Griffin).
2006/07  Joined the Chiswick and District Sunday Football League, Runners Up Chiswick & District
Premier Division and Runners Up League Cup (Bedfont Sunday), MCFA Premier Cup Semi-Finalists.
2005-06  3rd placed in final season in the West Middlesex League, Premier Cup semi-finalists
2004-05  Open Cup Finalists, Premier Cup Semi-Finalists.
2003-04  Open Cup Winners (Hayes End Wanderers 3-0), Bedfont Challenge Shield
winners, MCFA Premier Cup Semi-Finalists, Premier Cup Finalists.
2002-03  Champions for a seventh time, Bedfont Challenge Shield Winners and Premier Cup
runners up. Middlesex County Premier Cup Semi-Finalists.
2001-02  Middlesex County Premier Semi-Finalists
2000-01  3rd placed finish and Edmondson Premier Cup semi-finalists with the new team.
1999-00  Bedfont Shield runners up, 4th placed in the WMSFL Premier Division.
1998-99  Premier Division runners up and MCFA semi-finalists losing on penalties to
Domine Athletic who went on to win the trophy.
1997-98 A 6th title and Bedfont Shield Winners (Bedfont) 5-4 on penalties.
1996-97  Premier Division runners up, Open Cup winners (Domine Athletic 1-0) and
MCFA Semi-Finalists.
1995-96  5th Premier Division title and Open Cup Semi-Finalists.
1994-95 Premier Division runners up, Open Cup winners (Manor 3-1) and Bedfont Shield Winners (Bedfont Sunday 2-1)
1993-94  MCFA Premier Cup Quarter-finalists and 3rd in the Premier table.
1992-93  Albion regain title and MCFA semi-finalists losing 1-0 in a replay to Hammersmith
1991-92  3rd Premier Division title, four points clear of Sunbury Park, and MCFA Premier Cup
finalists losing 2-0 to West Hendon ESC.
1990-91  Open Cup Winners for the first time, defeating Ham Venturers 1-0 at Hounslow Town
1989-90  4th placed in the Premier Division.
1988-89  Premier Cup winners again defeating Heston Celtic 4-0.
1987-88  Premier Division runners up, and Premier Cup Winners for a 3rd successive season with 3-1
victory over Shepperton Sports
1986-87  Premier Division runners up and Premier Cup Winners with 2-1 victory over Premier champions Sunbury Park
1985-86  Premier Division and MCFA cup runners up both times to Sunbury Park.
Premier Cup Winners defeating Brook House 2-0.
1984-85  Middlesex Premier County Champions with 5-3 victory over Lucas CAV at Hayes FC.
1983-84  Wallis-Athenian Cup Winners with 1-0 victory against Cranford and first venture into the
FA National Cup.
1982-83 Champions again and Premier Cup Winners, defeating Cranford 2-1 our best ever season so far.
1981-82  MCFA Cup semi-finalists losing 1-0 to Arras, and Wallis-Athenian Cup runners up.
1980-81  At last the Premier Division title as we see off Sunbury Park by one point.
1979-80  Premier Cup runners up again to Southerners losing 2-0.
1978-79  Open Cup Semi-Finalists.
1977-78  First season in the Premier Division, Premier Cup runners up in replay with Southerners after a 3-3 draw.
1976-77  4th place in the Intermediate Division.
1975-76  First season in the WMSFL ends with the Division One title and reaching the 5th round of the MCFA Junior Cup.
1974-75  The story begins in the Chiswick and District Sunday League and runners up in the
Intermediate Division.
